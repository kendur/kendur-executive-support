# Origin and Context

**Date:** 2026-07-22  
**Status:** Established

## Origin

The project began as a private effort to build a knowledge-based task-management and scheduling system in Notion for the primary maintainer.

The practical problem was not a lack of goals or understanding. It was the recurring gap between intention and action: rapid idea loss, working-memory pressure, difficulty initiating tasks, time blindness, interruption, and the need to preserve enough context to resume later.

## Early design direction

The initial system combined ideas from GTD and PARA while rejecting the burden of deep nesting, extensive setup, and workflows that require tutorials.

Key requirements emerged:

- capture in seconds;
- keep task and project context together;
- support visually quiet, explicit interfaces;
- separate Personal and Work information while allowing a unified view;
- ingest conversations and meetings from multiple capture devices;
- use AI and automation when helpful without requiring them.

## Product shift

During the initial discussion, the project was recognized as a problem shared by other people. The scope changed from a permanently personal build to a private pilot for a potentially monetizable open product.

The complete manual Notion system would remain free. Future revenue would come from hosting, managed automation, optional intelligence, migrations, testing, and support.

## Relationship to Nexus

The larger Nexus concept had become overwhelming because it attempted to solve the complete personal operating-system problem at once. This project became the first bounded Nexus module: small enough to build and test, valuable enough to stand alone, and capable of proving the broader architecture.

## Open and source-agnostic direction

The project adopted two durable positions:

1. Community users may fork, modify, redistribute, and publish the system under attribution terms while official tested releases remain distinguishable.
2. Capture hardware and services are replaceable sources. The product owns normalization, context, clarification, action, scheduling, and recovery rather than depending on one recorder or AI provider.
