## Example processed output

### What you are trying to do

You want to build something for people who operate the way you do: capable, self-taught, and accustomed to figuring things out through trial and error, but often forced to spend too much energy reconstructing thoughts, skills, and context.

The goal is not to claim medical, psychological, or programming expertise. It is to turn lived experience into a practical system that may save other people—especially younger people with similar struggles—some of the time you lost fighting the same battles repeatedly.

### Three product decisions in the thought stream

1. **The useful core should be free and understandable without a tutorial.** A person should be able to duplicate the Notion template and begin using the basic capture-to-action workflow without technical knowledge.
2. **AI should act as an optional skill bridge.** A provider-agnostic AI Guide Pack should explain the template, ask only a few relevant questions at a time, and adjust its guidance to the user’s confidence and technical ability.
3. **Advanced help should appear only when needed.** The guide may help a user add automations, capture devices, agents, or integrations, but those features must not make the basic system feel incomplete or inaccessible.

### Product requirements extracted

- Preserve the raw capture and its provenance so the user can return to the original meaning.
- Separate capture from organization; do not require structure while the person is speaking.
- Segment long thought streams without erasing uncertainty, corrections, repetition, or unfinished threads.
- Use Personal and Work profiles rather than employer-specific terminology.
- Keep the core AI-optional and the guidance LLM-agnostic.
- Ask a short, progressive skill questionnaire rather than presenting a long setup interview.
- Adapt explanations to the user’s current knowledge and desired level of assistance.
- Help with setup, daily operation, maintenance, troubleshooting, integrations, and agent options.
- Keep diagnostic, treatment, and therapeutic claims outside the product.

### Unfinished threads preserved

- What questions should the first skill questionnaire ask?
- How will the AI Guide Pack know which template and prompt version is current?
- Which guidance belongs in the free/open project, and which value belongs in managed services?
- How should the guide explain privacy when users connect third-party AI providers?
- How should the system distinguish a passing thought from a decision or commitment?

### Suggested next action

Define the first AI Guide Pack as three small components rather than one large prompt:

1. a stable project-and-template reference;
2. a short adaptive skill questionnaire;
3. task-specific guides for setup, daily use, troubleshooting, and advanced integrations.
