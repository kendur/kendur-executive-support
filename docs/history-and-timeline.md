# Project History and Timeline

This document records how the Executive Support System reached its current shape so the repository can be
understood without outside context.

---

## Origin

The project began as a need for a low-friction way to turn incoming obligations into clear next actions across
Personal and Work profiles. Early thinking treated it as one layer of the broader **Nexus** personal operating
system concept rather than a standalone app idea.

As the documentation matured, the effort shifted from private workflow notes to a product-capable architecture:
manual first, Notion-centered, adapter-based, and suitable for public iteration.

---

## Timeline

- **Concept formed** — A capture-first support system was defined for handling obligations, notes, and follow-up
  across Personal and Work profiles.
- **Nexus relationship clarified** — The project was framed as the action-and-obligation layer of the broader
  Nexus concept.
- **Architecture broadened** — The design moved beyond a personal setup into a product-capable model with
  replaceable automation and AI adapters, while keeping a future first-party backend in scope.
- **Open community direction chosen** — The repository committed to a free, forkable Community edition with paid
  paths focused on services, hosting, and support rather than gating the core workflow.
- **Capture-source-agnostic strategy accepted** — Manual entry, email, raw audio, files, transcripts, and future
  sources were all treated as valid paths into one Inbox.

---

## Governance significance

These decisions explain why the repository is documentation-first, why Community remains the public baseline,
and why future implementation can expand from third-party adapters to first-party services without changing the
core promise.
